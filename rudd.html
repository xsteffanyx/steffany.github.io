Process

Designing for the Environment
According to the World Conservation Union, invasive alien species are the second biggest threat to biodiversity after habitat loss. Most introduced species fail to establish in new environments, but some thrive due to their rapid growth, high reproductive rates, and ability to outcompete native species for resources. These invaders often lack natural predators, making them difficult to control. Their impact on ecosystems, habitats, and native species can be severe and irreversible, costing billions annually. The mission for Rudd is to combat invasive species, reduce plastic pollution, and enhance Canada‚Äôs green spaces. Seed bombs perfectly stand with these goals by offering an innovative, eco-friendly, and accessible way to promote biodiversity and restore ecosystems.

The Product
Defining how to showcase the product itself, I decided create a flower and a grass for each ecological Ontario region, which are the Hudson's Bay Lowlands, the Ontario Shield, and the Mixedwood Plains. AFter extensive research on many native plants, the final plants were chosen for being pollinators, non-toxic/non-allergens, low maintenance, and aesthetics.

Why Rudd?
I chose the name "RUDD" for our seed bomb brand because it carries a warm, earthy tone that felt naturally aligned with our mission. Originally, I called it "Rudd Seeds," but dropping "seeds" gave the name more strength and simplicity. "Rudd" evokes the idea of a glowing, healthy complexion‚Äîsomething alive, vibrant, and grounded‚Äîwhich mirrors the life our seed bombs bring to the earth. It also has a soft, organic sound, reminiscent of words like "mudd," another name I considered, but Rudd stood out for its subtle connotation with beauty and a raw, natural feel.
