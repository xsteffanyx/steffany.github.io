Redesigning Better Homes & Gardens

2025 - OCAD YEAR 3

For this graphic design project, I was tasked with designing a custom Point of Purchase (POP) structural package that houses and protects a set of four fragile identical items using an original dieline I created. The main challenge was to engineer a structural form that reveals at least 30% of the contents using dynamic die-cuts, allowing consumers to both see and touch the product without relying on photography. I explored structural packaging principles through hands-on construction, scoring, and folding techniques, then applied these to design a sustainable, secure, and visually engaging package. Alongside the physical form, I also proposed a refreshed brand identity and created a visual business case analyzing the current market, competitors, and the logic behind my structural and branding decisions.

Process

Target Audience
Our redesigned POP packaging for Better Homes & Gardens drinking glasses is aimed at a broad yet defined audience of adults aged 19 to 55 who value both utility and aesthetic. These include home entertainers, cocktail enthusiasts, kitchen decor lovers, and individuals who appreciate well-crafted glassware. This new demographic seeks high-quality products with modern, minimal design and are drawn to lifestyle messaging that reflects taste and intention. They prioritize experiences over possessions, enjoy giving thoughtful gifts, and are increasingly conscious of sustainability in the products they purchase. Our packaging responds to these preferences by using simple, type-led design with minimal but effective graphics, avoiding excessive text or visual clutter. The clean layout and neutral color palette are intended to resonate with both younger and older consumers, presenting the product as elegant, timeless, and gift-worthy.

Brand Identity
Our structural packaging design reflects a new personality of the Better Homes & Gardens brand: sophisticated yet approachable, eco-conscious, and rooted in a blend of classic tradition and contemporary sensibility.
