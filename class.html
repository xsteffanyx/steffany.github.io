Inequality in the Classroom

2024 - OCAD YEAR 3

I designed and built a website that tells a compelling, data-driven story by combining quantitative visualization, qualitative diagrams, and rich visual storytelling. I began by identifying a topic that could benefit from both factual clarity and emotional engagement. Using charts, custom illustrations, and structured diagrams, I broke down complex ideas into accessible, digestible sections. Process

First I chose my topic which was Gender Inequality in Education, I wanted to make something informative with visual graphs showing these disparities. I knew I wanted to make a micro-website for this because it required a lot of information around culture and laws so I needed space to slowly inform the viewer that a poster, dashboard, or animation wouldn‚Äôt allow me to expand on. I focused the project on the consequences of these disparities in health with maternal and child mortality. I did so to create a cause and effect story and to make my story concise as my topic has many elements that could not be covered well with a limited length. I gathered my data from the World Bank - World Development Indicators and World Happiness Report, and gathered as many countries with entries of all four variables I had. I looked toward my moodboard to decide on the aesthetics of the website, keeping it simple and a colour palette connoting the topic discussed. I used Rawgraphs.io to develop the scatterplots then stylized on Illustrator, this graph was best for my data especially in the patterns it creates with the variables of happiness and mortality. I found information to support these patterns and add further explanation through various studies I found on PubMed Central. Images were found from the BBC news country profiles. Everything was coded on Visual Studio Code with html, css, and javascript.
