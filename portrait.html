Beached: Self-Portrait

I used the story of the Little Mermaid as a metaphor between my sense of identity and the ocean. Sirens draw in like the waves draw in whatever is on the shore and cleanses it, transforming with pressure. The composition is grounded in the rising of a mermaid coming from the waves with a new found strength but it lingers in hope, signifying change as neither heroic nor malignant. My methodology was informed by old myths and pop culture, because unlike the classic heroes, I want to depict the world in new gentler eyes, my goal is not to save the world but to save myself. The skin colour taken from manatees and the pose from the Disney film, invoking memories of particularly my childhood and the mysteries within it. I wanted to create a piece that was its own mini-fairy tale, giving a conflicting feeling of solitariness but gushing with intimacy. As a slight nude the portrait exposes a rawness that lies deeper than the emotion shown in the subject, still a churning contemplation maintains a constant in the piece. I believe we seek meaning in life, but what we actually seek is an experience of being alive. To have feet to walk, dance, and move on.

Beached. 24x18 in. Acrylic Paint and Glitter on Panel.
