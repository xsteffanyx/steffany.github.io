Passiflora

2024 - OCAD YEAR 3

For this project, I was tasked with designing a brand identity based on the personality and values of a classmate. Through a series of in-depth conversations, I gathered insights into my classmate Laura's character traits, beliefs, passions, and aesthetics. I synthesized this information into three core attributes that formed the foundation of their personal brand. From there, I developed a brand name that symbolized their essence, selected a typeface that reflected their tone and presence, and crafted supporting visual elements including color, texture/graphics, and modifications to typography. This project challenged me to transform abstract human qualities into a clear, expressive, and authentic visual identity.

Process

Defining Laura
The Process of Turning Laura into a Brand: This section of my process delves into the steps I took to turn Laura into a brand. Starting off with defining her attributes as a person from the interview, turning those traits into a visual mood-board, and lastly finding a name and style which combines all this research into a cohesive brand.

Warm. (showing or expressive of enthusiasm, affection, or kindness.)
Multifaceted. (full of complicated details, having intricate patterns and motifs)
Passionate. (having or showing strong feelings of enthusiasm or belief)
I believe these words capture Laura in essence and are excellent for branding because they evoke powerful emotional and psychological responses. They makes the brand feel human, creates a sense of authenticity and versatility.
