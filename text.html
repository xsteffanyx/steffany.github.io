Artist Statement

Children have a profound understanding of love and fear, more pure and unfiltered than adults. Maturity has only brought me confusion, too many complexities. I long for the days I thought in black and white.


Process

Honoring Fanshawe Park: Growing up in London, Ontario, was a bit complicated, especially with it becoming a second rate Toronto. So I gave myself the challenge to recall the best parts of that childhood and honor the city for it‚Äôs best parts. I sat for a while with this feeling of nostalgia, I missed lonely hikes to nowhere at Fanshawe Park. I remember summers walking, running, camping, swimming, fishing, biking, and playing. With this I began my type installation with the concepts of play, childhood, and nature (specifically the river and the trees).

Rationale on Material In the many examples shown in class, my favourites were the ones that became one with the medium and environment. I thought of natural materials, wood, metal, and rocks, of materials that would not hurt the environment (as it is a conservation area). I had an idea of a textile installation, of cotton or linen, that, with a lot of time, would go back into nature. There are a myriad of type of textiles, but keeping childhood in mind, I thought of lace and doilies, reminiscent of homey, kitschy decor of a grandmother‚Äôs house.

Gathering Inspiration My inspiration research came from past knowledge of textile installation, Sheila Hicks greatly influenced this assignment. I looked towards what others were doing with fabric and yarn, from other installations to personal projects. In class feedback, I was introduced to Christo and Jeanne-Claude, which allowed me to think much larger in scale. Throughout all these works, the most I enjoyed is the transparency of the medium, I sought out to take bits of from each, replicate and transform them into the installations I make.
